🔄 Biopsychosocial Model: Barriers to Community Physiotherapy Attendance

Use the community stage to explore why Mr. Ahmed stopped attending exercise classes. Ask him about these factors:

🫀 BIOLOGICAL Factors

  • Persistent pain & swelling: Knee pain 4-6/10, evening swelling, stiffness — reduces capacity and motivation to exercise
  • Deconditioning: Lost muscle mass during infection, reduced cardiovascular fitness — cannot keep up with class intensity
  • Fatigue: Poor sleep (4-5 hrs/night), diabetes, post-infection recovery — insufficient energy for exercise
  • Fear of falling: Nearly fell on wet stairs, feels unsteady — kinesiophobia (fear of movement) prevents engagement
  • Comorbidities: Diabetes (poor control, no test strips), hypertension, obesity — complex health needs not addressed in generic class
  • Worry about infection recurrence: Checks wound daily, anxious about exertion — believes rest is safer than exercise

🧠 PSYCHOLOGICAL Factors

  • Depression & low mood: Cries alone, anhedonia (no enjoyment), feels "useless" — reduced motivation and self-efficacy
  • Social anxiety: Felt judged, embarrassed, humiliated at class — anticipatory anxiety prevents return
  • Loss of confidence: Formerly strong warehouse worker, now dependent on crutch — identity crisis and shame
  • Cognitive impairment: Forgetful, confused by leaflet, worries about appearing "stupid" — health literacy barrier
  • Fear-avoidance: Worries about dislocating implant, infection recurrence, breaking hip — catastrophic thinking
  • Grief & bereavement: Wife was carer/translator/motivator — her loss removed his support system and purpose
  • Stigma & izzat: Cultural honour/shame — men should be strong, independent; using crutch = loss of dignity

👥 SOCIAL Factors

  • Financial deprivation: £180/week pension, £8 return bus fare = 4.4% of weekly income — transport unaffordable
  • Social isolation: Days without speaking to anyone, no friends, no mosque — no "exercise buddy" or social motivation
  • Cultural mismatch: Class was all white older women, loud music, fast English — no sense of belonging
  • Language barrier: Could not understand instructions ("engage your core", "feel the burn") — too embarrassed to ask
  • Religious needs: No prayer room, no halal food, class times clashed with prayer — environment not inclusive
  • Inadequate housing: 2nd floor flat, no lift, 24 stairs, small bathroom — cannot practise exercises at home safely
  • Health literacy: Leaflet too complex, no internet, no one to check technique — cannot self-manage
  • Family burden: Daughter exhausted (3 children, part-time work), son distant — no one to transport/accompany him
  • Discrimination/perceived exclusion: Felt invisible, instructor ignored him, no one spoke to him — reinforced isolation
  • Loss of work identity: 35-year warehouse career gave purpose, friends, routine — retirement removed all structure

💡 CLINICAL REASONING QUESTIONS

  • How do these biological, psychological, and social factors INTERACT to create a barrier cycle?
  • Which single intervention would have the GREATEST impact on attendance? (e.g., home visits vs. transport vouchers vs. culturally adapted classes)
  • What is the role of the physiotherapist in addressing non-physical barriers? (e.g., signposting to social prescribing, link workers, benefits advice)
  • How would you apply the COM-B model (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation → Behaviour) to Mr. Ahmed?
  • What MDT referrals are needed? (OT, social services, diabetes team, mental health, benefits advice, faith/community link worker)
  • How does this case illustrate health inequity — and what system-level changes could prevent it?
